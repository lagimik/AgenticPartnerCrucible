Excellent report by Deloitte Engineering pointing to four main reasons why enterprises are failing to to roll out AI Agents. Most enterprises are stuck in a large “agent scaling gap.” AI can technically do the work, but companies struggle to deploy it at scale. Only ~25% of organizations have moved a significant portion of AI experiments into production. Why? The report highlights four recurring failure points: 🧠 The organizational immune response Companies try to layer agents onto human-designed workflows instead of redesigning processes for autonomy. ⚙️ Probabilistic AI meets deterministic systems Agents reason probabilistically, while enterprise systems expect perfect inputs. Without validation layers and guardrails, reliability breaks down. 📚 The “dumb RAG” trap Dumping documents into a vector database ≠ organizational knowledge. Retrieval architecture and governance matter more than most teams realize. ⚖️ The accountability vacuum When an agent makes a costly mistake, who owns the decision? Without clear ownership, the first failure often shuts the entire program down. One statistic that explains a lot: 💰 Enterprises spend ~93% of AI budgets on technology and only ~7% on people and process redesign. That’s backwards. Scaling AI agents isn’t primarily a technology challenge. It’s a trust, architecture, and operating model challenge. The companies that win won’t just build better agents. They’ll redesign how work gets done. 🚀

The 93/7 split tells you everything. The immune response isn't resistance. It's a rational reaction to an environment that still rewards the old workflow. The accountability vacuum isn't an oversight. It's what happens when nobody defines decision rights before the agent goes live. The 7% isn't just underfunded. It's sequenced wrong. Most of it lands after deployment, when behavioral patterns have already formed around the gaps. By then, you're not designing the conditions. You're trying to displace a system nobody designed. Good work by the Deloitte Engineering team for naming this clearly.
